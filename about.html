Bihar Dalit Vikas Samiti (BDVS)

Empowering Dalit Communities Since 1982

History & Background

Bihar Dalit Vikas Samiti (BDVS) is a community-based organization that began in 1982 as an initiative by the Jesuits of Patna Province. Originally a project under Navjyoti Niketan in Patna, BDVS became an independent registered society in 1994. Its mission was shaped by the Jesuits' reflection on the socio-economic and cultural marginalization of Dalits in Bihar, particularly after India's Emergency period (1975â€“77).

BDVS works directly with Dalit communities to enhance self-reliance, build community-based organizations, and promote socio-political and economic awareness. Operating across 13 districts of Bihar, BDVS has evolved into a movement empowering Dalits through education, women's leadership, sustainable livelihood, and legal awareness.

Over time, BDVS shifted from a purely welfare-oriented approach to a rights-based, participatory model. This change emphasized community involvement and grassroots leadership. Today, BDVS is a beacon of hope for Dalit communities, especially Musahars, providing support in areas like primary education, child parliament, food security, women empowerment, and disaster response.

BDVSâ€™s journey from a small intervention to a large social movement reflects the commitment of Jesuits and local leaders. Its dedicated work in some of Biharâ€™s most deprived regions stands as a testimony to sustainable and inclusive development.

About Bihar Dalit Vikas Samiti

Building Dignity, Justice & Transformation

Bihar Dalit Vikas Samiti (BDVS) is not just an NGOâ€”it is a grassroots movement born from the dreams, courage, and resilience of the Dalit community in Bihar. Since 1982, we have been working to ensure that every child learns, every woman leads, and every family lives with dignity and justice.

Established by Fr. Jose Kananaikil, SJ, BDVS was envisioned as a platform where marginalized voices could rise and demand their rights. At a time when Dalits faced systemic exclusion, BDVS created safe spaces for leadership, education, and collective action.

Over the decades, BDVS has mobilized 30,000+ families across 13 districts of Bihar, driving transformation in education, livelihoods, gender equality, governance, and community leadership.
