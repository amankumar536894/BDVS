Capacity Building Training

Empowering Dalit Women through Leadership & Entrepreneurship

Venue: BDVS Administrative Office, Patna | Dates: November 27–29, 2024

A three-day Capacity Building Training was organized for Dalit women to strengthen their leadership, entrepreneurship, and financial management skills. The sessions included hands-on workshops on cash book maintenance, ledger management, fundraising, and project management, ensuring transparency and accountability in grassroots organizations.

The program encouraged women to explore small businesses in handicrafts and sewing, promoting economic independence and dignity within their communities.

Key Highlights

Skills Development

Training in project management, entrepreneurship, financial literacy, and leadership through practical workshops.

Entrepreneurship Focus

Encouraging women to manage small businesses like handicrafts and tailoring for economic self-reliance.

Leadership & Governance

Workshops helped participants take leadership roles in their communities and federations.

Impact

  • 50 women equipped with essential leadership skills.
  • Improved financial literacy – budgeting, bookkeeping, and fundraising.
  • Entrepreneurial empowerment to start and manage businesses.
  • Promoting sustainable development & gender equality.
Advanced Embroidery Training

Empowering Dalit Women Tailors Through Skill Development

An initiative to build confidence, enhance craft, and promote economic independence among Dalit women.

From November 27–29, 2024, an enriching embroidery training program was held at the BDVS Administrative Office, Patna. The training was led by USHA trainers Mr. Santosh Kumar and Mr. Manedranath, focusing on advanced embroidery techniques and entrepreneurship skills.

  • Basic to Advanced Stitches
  • Colourful Thread Techniques
  • Decorative Embroidery for Apparel & Home Décor

Beyond technical skills, participants gained insights into marketing, business management, and entrepreneurship. The program encouraged women to envision a sustainable future in the handicraft industry.

Community Impact

This initiative empowered women with not just craft but also confidence and connections. It became a platform for networking, peer support, and collective growth. By the end of the training, participants were equipped to launch or enhance their own tailoring businesses, breaking barriers and building a better future.

“Through embroidery, we are not just creating designs — we are weaving dignity, independence, and transformation.”

Convention of Dalit Organizations

State-Level Convention of Village Level Organizations

Organized by Bihar Dalit Vikas Samiti (BDVS) from October 1–3, 2024 at Maa Kali Marriage Hall, Patna. Over 304 participants joined to strengthen leadership, rights awareness, and grassroots governance for Dalit communities across 10 districts.

Overview

The convention empowered Village Level Organizations (VLOs) through leadership development, rights awareness, cultural identity, and collective advocacy. It marked significant women and youth participation, showcasing the growing inclusivity of grassroots movements in Bihar.

Key Outcomes

200+ participants trained in grassroots leadership and governance.

Improved VLO functioning in 90% units across districts.

Awareness created on key schemes like E-Shram, Ayushman Bharat, PDS.

Cultural performances integrated with community learning and advocacy.

Challenges Noted

  • Limited digital access and awareness of government schemes.
  • Weak Gram Sabha participation in several areas.
  • Persistent social discrimination and marginalization.

Recommendations

To ensure sustainable progress, BDVS emphasized the need for regular VLO trainings, stronger digital infrastructure at village levels, inter-VLO collaboration, and continued focus on empowering women and youth as leaders of change.

Convention Highlights

“The convention reinvigorated grassroots efforts, equipping communities with tools and resolve to lead inclusive and sustainable development in Bihar.”

Bihar Dalit Vikas Samiti

State-Level Convention of Ambedkar Yuva Manch (AYM)

Empowering Dalit youth through leadership, skills, and social justice initiatives.

About the Convention

The State-Level Convention of Ambedkar Yuva Manch (AYM), organized by BDVS in Patna on October 21–22, 2024, brought together 289 youth leaders from across Bihar. With the presence of Shri Ratnesh Sada (Minister, Bihar Govt.) and Mr. Vijay Prakash (Ex-IAS), the event focused on youth empowerment, skill development, and Ambedkarite values of justice and equality.

  • Inaugural session with flag hoisting & welcome song
  • Keynote on education, digital literacy & entrepreneurship
  • Cultural skit: “Nashe ko Na, Shiksha ko Haan”
  • Capacity building, youth schemes & RTI training

Maa Kali Marriage Hall, Bihar

Key Highlights

The convention emphasized youth leadership, digital inclusion, skill-based livelihoods, and safe migration practices. Inspiring success stories of AYM members like Rakesh Kumar (Bihar Police), Rupak Kumar (Entrepreneur), and Sanjeet Kumar (Digital Services) highlighted the power of grassroots transformation.
